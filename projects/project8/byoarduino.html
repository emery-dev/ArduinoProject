Build Your Own Arduino

About

At about $30, the Arduino isn't an expensive device, but if you want to make your own, simpler version for around $5.

Information

Difficulty: Medium

Estimated time: 1-2 hours

Story

We will learn about the workings of electricity and the Arduino microcontroller by building our own! This is a fairly simple project that will introduce you to electricity. But remember that the Arduino that we build only work because the Arduino bootloader is burned onto the ATMEGA chip.

Components

The following hardware components were used in this project:

  • 1x ATMEGA328p with oscillator and bootloader
  • 1x 5V power circuit
  • 1x FTDI serial connector
  • 2x (14 pin) of headers
  • Wiring (small, preferably)

Instructions

Following the schematics above, first Put the ATMega328 in the breadboard, with the little semicircular cutout facing one end of the breadboard.

Then, Put the resonator in the breadboard in the remaining three pins under the ATMega328, direction doesnâ€™t matter.

As in Figure 3, wire up the power as shown. The green wire is +5v, the blue one is GND. They are supposed to cross, yes.

Then wire the resonator to the two XTAL pins, the GND pin of the resonator should go the GND. See picture.

Put the 10kOhm resistor between +5v and the upper-left pin on the ATMega328 (reset). This will pull it high, and keep it from resetting itself.

Add the LED between GND and the pin two spaces after GND, see picture. The shorter lead of the LED (-) goes to GND. You will be able to activate this LED by pulling Arduino Pin 13 HIGH.
